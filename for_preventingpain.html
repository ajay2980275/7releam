Chronic pain. It's Real. It's Preventable. Learn How.

Chronic pain is the #1 reason for seeking care, #1 cause of disability and addiction, and #1 driver of health care costs, costing more than cancer, heart disease, and diabetes (Institute of Medicine, 2011). There are hundreds of risk factors that can lead to chronic pain. By taking some simple steps each day, pain can be relieved and prevented.

Preventing Chronic Pain

Engage

 

The U.S National Pain Strategy states that people with pain need to be engaged to improve their pain through patient-centered integrated care services that is based on understanding and caring for the whole person. This is a key factor in transformative care.

Educate

 

The Campaign for Preventing Chronic Pain states that pain conditions are best managed by training people to reduce lifestyle risk factors that cause pain and strengthen protective factors that can help prevent pain. This is a key factor in transformative care.

Empower

 

The Institute of Medicine states that health professionals’ primary role in caring for chronic pain requires guiding, coaching, and assisting patients with day-to-day self-management in addition to evidence based biomedical treatments. This is a key factor in transformative care.

Headache

Daily caffeine from coffee and poor sleep was leading to headaches. They went away when she learned to change her risk factors.

 

Jaw Pain

Teeth clenching in response to stress caused his jaw pain and headaches. They went away when he learned how to change these risk factors.

 

Neck Pain

A car accident caused his pain. The pain went away when he worked on stretching exercise, posture, and stress at work.

 

Hip Pain

Sleeping on soft bed caused his hip pain. The pain went away when he got a new bed, began exercising, and changed his posture at work.

 

Shoulder Pain

Carrying a heavy backpack to school started her pain. The pain went away when she learned exercises and changed her backpack.

 

Back Pain

Lifting at work started his pain. The pain went away when he began yoga exercise, improved how he used his back, and decreased stress at work.

 

Ear Pain

Yawning wide brought on the pain when the jaw snapped. The pain went away when she learned exercises, and reduced clenching and gum chewing.

 

Tooth Pain

Teeth sensivity came on after stress of family problems. The pain was improved by learning to calm her anxiety and relax the jaw to reduce strain in the teeth.
